## 2026-04-26 - Screen Reader Issues with Material Symbols Ligatures
**Learning:** This application heavily relies on Material Symbols font ligatures for iconography (e.g., `<span class="material-symbols-outlined">rocket_launch</span>`). Without `aria-hidden="true"`, screen readers will read the raw text of the ligature (like "rocket underscore launch") rather than interpreting it as an icon, leading to a confusing and broken accessibility experience. Additionally, many functional buttons on responsive layouts hide their text labels on small screens, meaning they act as icon-only buttons on mobile devices.
**Action:** Always add `aria-hidden="true"` to structural icon elements (like Material Symbols and SVGs) to hide them from assistive technologies. Furthermore, any interactive element (like `<button>` or `<a>`) that relies on an icon for its visual meaning—especially when text is hidden on small viewports—must have an explicit, descriptive `aria-label` attribute to provide context to screen reader users.
